What are in_channels, out_channels, kernel_size and stride?

Our data's dimension is: (number of images, 28, 28, 1), which are (28, 28) images and has only 1 channel of color. Note that a color image normally has 3 channels: red, green and blue. However, here we're only working with 1 channel of color, something like this:
Thus, our in_channels would be 1.

Next, out_channel is the number of channels produced by the convolution, or basically the number of filters that will be trained for this Conv layers. TODO, more illustration here?

kernel_size is the size of the kernels that will be `convolved` around the image. kernel_size, padding and stride will determine the size of the feature maps produced by the convolution operation while output_channel will determine the number of feature maps.

stride is, in simple terms, the number of pixels the convolution will skip for each step.
